If you’re behind on your AEC industry reading, check out Architizer’s recently published article, “Blending New Projects Into Historic London With Vectorworks.”

![Yurky Cross.jpg](https://blog.vectorworks.net/hs-fs/hubfs/Blog%20Images/171204/Yurky%20Cross.jpg?width=500&name=Yurky%20Cross.jpg "Yurky Cross.jpg")

_A 3D model of a mixed-use project on Lewisham High Street; image courtesy of Yurky Cross Chartered Architects._

The article features [Yurky Cross Chartered Architects](http://yurkycross.co.uk/), a London-based firm that specializes in affordable housing and mixed-use projects in the South East. The firm often faces two reoccurring challenges with new projects in the city: space and differing opinions. Yurky Cross Chartered Architects’ Practice Director Michael Cross and Senior Architectural Technician Christiaan Briggs reveal how they solve these issues with examples from real-world projects.

Read the article to learn how [Vectorworks Architect software](http://www.vectorworks.net/architect?utm%5Fcampaign=blog&utm%5Fcontent=yurkycrossteaser120417&utm%5Fmedium=planetvectorworks&utm%5Fsource=intext) aids in their solution, why Camera Match is one of Briggs’ favorite features, and how [project sharing](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fIhwF6BuKok&t=32s) allows “for better, quicker teamwork in the office and with those remotely contributing.”

As a bonus, you can also access our “Customizable BIM Tools Enable Robust Workflows” article by entering your contact info in the form at the end of the Architizer story. Authored by Luc Lefebvre with insights from Richard Garber, AIA, principal of GRO Architects, this resource shares strategies for overcoming obstacles when customizing BIM workflows.
